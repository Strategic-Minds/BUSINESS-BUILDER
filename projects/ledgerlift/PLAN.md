## Phase Objective

Turn LedgerLift from concept into a governed execution path for discovery, brand, approval, docs, build, validation, and release. This PLAN phase defines the operating scope, assumptions, phase outputs, gating points, and immediate next actions.

## Business Concept

LedgerLift is an AI-powered back-office service for independent gyms in Austin. It helps gym owners automate three high-value operating workflows: member onboarding, billing follow-up, and weekly performance reporting.

Primary buyer: independent gym owners and small fitness studio operators in Austin who lack a dedicated operations or finance team.

Core promise: reduce administrative drag, protect cash collection, and give owners a clear weekly view of membership, revenue, and retention signals.

## End-to-End Workflow

7. VALIDATE: Verify documents, repository outputs, automation assumptions, QA evidence, and release readiness.
8. RELEASE: Prepare release packet and proceed only after approval for launch or production-impacting actions.

## Discovery Agenda

The DISCOVERY phase should answer:

- How many independent gyms and fitness studios in Austin are likely reachable and serviceable?
- Which gym management systems are commonly used by the target customer?
- Where do onboarding, billing follow-up, and reporting workflows break down for small operators?
- What competitors serve the same jobs: gym management software, bookkeepers, virtual assistants, revenue ops consultants, and AI automation agencies?
- What service packages and price points are credible for a local AI back-office service?
- What data, privacy, consent, and payment-process constraints must be reflected before build?
- What minimum viable workflow can be launched without overbuilding?

## Initial Product Hypothesis

Offer LedgerLift as a managed AI operations service rather than only a software product. Start with a narrow stack: onboarding sequence automation, failed-payment follow-up, overdue account triage, weekly owner report, and monthly optimization review.

Suggested initial packages to test in discovery:

- Starter Ops: onboarding automation plus weekly report.
- Revenue Guard: billing follow-up plus owner alerting.
- Full Back Office Lift: onboarding, billing follow-up, reporting, and monthly workflow optimization.

## Risks And Watchouts

- Gym management software integrations may vary widely by platform.
- Billing follow-up touches sensitive payment and customer relationship workflows.
- Owners may want outcomes rather than another dashboard.
- Local service positioning must avoid sounding like generic AI automation.
- Validation must include workflow reliability, message quality, privacy boundaries, and owner usability.

## Approval Gates

No approval needed for PLAN and read-only discovery.

Approval required before:

- Publishing or deploying any public website or live automation.
- Creating production accounts or provisioning live infrastructure.
- Sending outreach, social posts, billing messages, or customer-facing communication.
- Modifying or deleting important existing Drive or GitHub assets.
- Treating brand direction, pricing, or final build scope as locked.
